# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard: Employee Attrition Analysis

## 🚀 Project Overview
This Power BI project provides a data-driven approach to understanding employee turnover. By analyzing demographic, financial, and performance-related factors, this dashboard identifies the key drivers of attrition within the organization, enabling the HR department to take proactive measures in talent retention.

### 🎯 Objective
To identify patterns in employee attrition and provide actionable insights to reduce turnover rates and improve overall workforce stability.

---

## 📈 Key Insights (Executive Summary)
Based on the analysis of **1,470 employees**, the following critical findings were identified:

### **1. Core KPIs**
* **Total Employees:** 1,470
* **Attrition Count:** 237
* **Attrition Rate:** **16.1%**
* **Average Age:** 37 Years
* **Average Monthly Salary:** $6.5K
* **Average Tenure:** 7.0 Years

### **2. Demographic Drivers**
* **Age Group:** The **26-35 age bracket** has the highest attrition (116 employees), suggesting a need for better engagement for early-to-mid career professionals.
* **Gender:** Male employees represent a higher portion of attrition (**150**) compared to Female employees (**87**).
* **Education:** Employees from **Life Sciences** (38%) and **Medical** (27%) backgrounds contribute most to the turnover numbers.

### **3. Job Role & Satisfaction**
* **High-Risk Roles:** **Laboratory Technicians (62)**, **Sales Executives (57)**, and **Research Scientists (47)** are the top roles leaving the company.
* **Salary Impact:** A significant portion of attrition occurs in the lower salary bracket (**Upto 5k**), accounting for **163 out of 237** total exits.
* **Job Satisfaction:** A direct correlation was found between low Job Satisfaction scores (Rating 1 & 2) and the likelihood of leaving.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Features
* **Tool:** Power BI Desktop
* **Data Source:** CSV (HR Analytics Data)
* **Data Cleaning:** Power Query (Used for handling nulls, formatting currency, and creating conditional columns).
* **DAX Formulas:** Used for calculating Attrition Rate, Average Salary, and dynamic KPI cards.
* **Key Features:**
    * Interactive Slicers (Department, Education Field).
    * Cross-filtering between visuals (Age Group vs. Attrition).
    * Matrix tables for Job Role satisfaction analysis.

## 💡 Recommendations
1. **Competitive Compensation:** Since the majority of attrition happens in the **Upto 5k salary slab**, a review of entry-level and mid-level compensation packages is recommended.
2. **Career Pathing:** Focus on the **26-35 age group** by providing clear promotion paths and mentorship programs to improve long-term retention.
3. **Role-Specific Interventions:** High turnover in **Sales and Lab roles** suggests the need for work-life balance reviews or workload optimization in those specific departments.
